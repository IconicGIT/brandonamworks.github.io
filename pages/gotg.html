Guardians of the Galaxy: Battle for the Universe

About the Game

Guardians of the Galaxy: Battle for the Universe is a game developed by our class in year 3 of UPC: Videogame Design and Development Degree using the game engine Wiwa Engine. The game is a twin-stick bullet hell roguelite based on the universe of Guardians of the Galaxy, from Marvel comics.

The game features procedural generation to create its rooms and an item system to upgrade and change the player's stats and abilities.






My contribution

During the develpment of the game I was in charge of creating the particles stated within the Game Design Document, due to my prior responsability to create the Particle System for the engine.

In order to do so I worked along side with peers present both in design department and the art department with the objective to make the particles as similar as possible to the references the GDD stated. This included the creation of the textures by part of the artist and the necessary shaders by part of me using GLSL.

Particle Emitter Editor panel

The first part of the editor lets the developer customize the base parameters of the particle emitter, such as the mesh or materials used, if the emitter is active, the particle spawning amount or the maximum number of particles to work with.

The second part lets the developer customize the behavior of the particles. The behaviors are divided between Position & Translation, Rotation & Angular Velocity, Scale & Growth and Color & Blending.
